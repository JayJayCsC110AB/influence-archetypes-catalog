Zendaya does the same on red carpets, where each outfit is treated as a cultural event, dissected and shared millions of times within hours. Both understood that looking undeniably different from everyone else is itself a form of content.

Marie Antoinette kept the public obsessed through constant speculation about her private life, her spending, and her politics — people couldn't look away because they never knew what she would do next. Zendaya sustains engagement the same way, moving between acting, fashion, and music in ways that keep audiences guessing what she'll do next. In both cases retention is driven by a persona that feels layered — there's always something more to discover.
  • Distribution:Marie Antoinette's influence spread through pamphlets, portraits, and word of mouth across European courts, where her style was copied by noblewomen trying to replicate Versailles. Zendaya distributes her influence through Instagram, red carpet coverage, and brand partnerships that push her image across every platform simultaneously. The mechanism is different but the result is the same — a single person's aesthetic choices become a template that thousands of people follow.
